Personal Website

Role: Designer & Developer HTML / CSS JavaScript Figma

A fully self-coded personal portfolio website designed and developed from scratch. The goal was to create a professional digital presence that reflects my design sensibility — clean, structured, and human-centered — while demonstrating my front-end development skills through a fully custom codebase with no templates.

View live site Hosted on GitHub Pages
Visit Site ↗

My Role & Contributions

I was responsible for the full project — from initial concept and wireframes through to final coded output. I made all layout and typography decisions, handled the visual hierarchy, wrote every line of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and deployed the site to GitHub Pages. The design was driven by the same brand principles I apply across all my work: clear, accessible, and structured.

Design Process

01
Research

I studied portfolio sites from UX designers and front-end developers to understand conventions around navigation, content hierarchy, and how to communicate skills effectively to a hiring audience.

02
Wireframes

I wireframed all page layouts in Figma — mapping out the homepage, about, portfolio, and contact sections across desktop and mid-size breakpoints before writing any code.

03
Prototype & Build

I connected the frames into an interactive prototype in Figma to test navigation flows, then translated the approved design into a fully coded, responsive multi-page site.

Figma — Lo-fi Wireframes
Figma — Interactive Prototype Flows

Reflection

Building this site taught me how much design and development inform each other. Decisions I thought were purely visual — spacing, font weight, layout rhythm — had a direct impact on how readable and navigable the final product felt. Given more time, I would add more motion and interactivity to bring the experience to life further. This project gave me confidence in my ability to take a design from idea to deployed product entirely on my own.

CMS Clothing Store

Role: UI Designer Shopify Branding E-Commerce UX

CMS (Thrifted Vintage) is a clothing e-commerce store built on Shopify. The project focused on building a cohesive brand identity — a distinctive logo with a red maple motif, a dark monochromatic aesthetic, and a shopping experience that feels premium yet approachable for vintage streetwear shoppers.

View live store Password required: Teach-1234
Visit Store ↗

My Role & Contributions

I handled the full visual design and brand identity for CMS — including logo design, colour palette selection, typography choices, and Shopify theme customization. I structured the product layout to guide users through the catalog clearly, and made deliberate UI decisions to communicate a premium thrift aesthetic that resonates with the target demographic.

Design Process

01
Target Audience

The store targets young adults aged 18–30 interested in streetwear and sustainable fashion. I researched their visual preferences — raw, editorial imagery, dark tones, and minimal clutter — to inform every design decision.

02
Brand & Visual Identity

The CMS logo uses a bold red maple motif to signal Canadian roots, paired with a dark monochromatic palette and clean sans-serif typography. The result is a brand that feels established and intentional rather than DIY.

03
Shopify Build

I customized a Shopify theme to match the brand — adjusting layout sections, product grids, navigation structure, and typography settings to align with the visual system I had defined.

Reflection

This project pushed me to think about UI design in a commercial context — where every visual decision has to support a business goal. Designing within Shopify's constraints was a useful challenge: it forced me to work systematically within an existing system rather than building from scratch. I'm proud of the brand identity I developed, and if I were to revisit it, I would push the editorial photography direction further to make the store feel even more distinct.

Discover Cancún

Role: Designer & Developer WordPress HTML / CSS UX Research

Discover Cancún is a travel website designed to inspire visitors to explore the destination. The project features a vibrant, colourful brand identity, an immersive hero section with aerial photography, a search function, gallery, and curated destination content — all designed to make trip planning feel exciting and effortless.

View live site Hosted on Pantheon
Visit Site ↗

My Role & Contributions

I designed and developed the full site — creating the Cancún wordmark, selecting the colourful multi-toned brand palette, building the layout structure in WordPress, and writing custom CSS to achieve the visual design. I also structured the information architecture to guide users from inspiration through to practical destination details.

Design Process

01
User Research

I researched how people browse travel sites — prioritizing high-impact visuals, quick access to key information, and intuitive search. The target user is someone planning a first trip to Cancún looking for inspiration and practical guidance.

02
Information Architecture

I structured the site around three core user needs: inspiration (hero and gallery), information (about and highlights), and action (search and booking). Navigation was kept minimal to reduce friction.

03
Final Build

The finished site uses aerial photography, bold typography, and a vibrant multi-colour palette to evoke the energy of the destination. I'm most proud of the hero section and the custom Cancún wordmark that anchors the brand.

Reflection

This project challenged me to design for a very different visual tone than my usual minimal aesthetic — bold, colourful, and expressive. Working in WordPress taught me how to extend a platform through custom CSS while working within its structural constraints. Looking back, I would invest more time in the mobile responsiveness and would refine the typography hierarchy to create a clearer reading experience on smaller screens.
